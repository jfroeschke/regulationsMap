Shrimp Management Areas

Shrimp lobster management

Four species of shrimp are managed by the Gulf Council; brown shrimp, white shrimp, pink shrimp, and royal red shrimp. Brown shrimp, white shrimp, and pink shrimp have similar habitat requirements and use estuarine environments as juveniles then move out of the estuaries onto soft bottom and shell habitats in the Gulf of Mexico. Royal red shrimp occur in much deeper waters and can be associated with reef habitats.
Management of these species has occurred since 1981 and management measures are similar to those of other fisheries (i.e. closed areas, closed seasons, catch limits), but there is also a moratorium on the issuance of new shrimp fishing permits. More management information for shrimp can be found here. (http://gulfcouncil.org/fishery-management/implemented-plans/shrimp/)
