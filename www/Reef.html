Gulf of Mexico Reef Fish

The Gulf of Mexico Fishery Management Council manages 31 species of reef fishes. This includes highly reef associated species like black grouper as well as more transient species like greater amberjack. The first management action on these species was completed in 1984, since then there has been a multitude of management measures to promote sustainability, promote fair and equitable access, and protect habitat critical for reef fishes.

The Council in coordination with the National Marine Fisheries Service has identified several areas of the Gulf that warrant regulation including gear restrictions and prohibition of activities to protect important fish habitat as well as the fish stocks and humans that benefit from this resource. Additional information about reef fish managament areas is below. More information on reef fish management by the Gulf Council can also be found here: Reef Fish Management

Alabama Special Management Zone (SMZ)

For vessels operating as a charter vessel or headboat, a vessel that does not have a commercial permit for Gulf of Mexico reef fish, or a vessel with such a permit fishing for Gulf of Mexico reef fish, fishing is limited to hook-and-line gear with no more than three hooks. Nonconforming gear is restricted to recreational bag limits, or for reef fish without a bag limit, to 5% by weight of all fish aboard.

Longline Buoy Gear Closure

Permanent closure to use of these gears for reef fish harvest inshore of 118 feet (36.6 meters) off the Florida shelf and inshore of 293 feet (91.4 meters) for the remainder of the Gulf, and encompasses 72,300 square nautical miles (nm2) or 133,344 km2 (GMFMC 1989). Bottom longline gear is prohibited inshore of 35 fathoms (54.3 meters) during the months of June through August in the eastern Gulf (GMFMC 2009), but is not depicted in Figure 3.2.2.

Madison Swanson

No-take marine reserves (total area is 219 nm2 or 405 km2 ) sited based on gag spawning aggregation areas where all fishing is prohibited except surface trolling from May through October (GMFMC 1999; 2003).

Reef Fish Stressed Area

A powerhead may not be used in the stressed area to take Gulf reef fish. A roller trawl may not be used in the stressed area.

The Edges

All fishing is prohibited in this area (390 nm2 or 1,338 km2) from January through April and possession of any fish species is prohibited, except for such possession aboard a vessel in transit with fishing gear stowed as specified. The provisions of this do not apply to highly migratory species (GMFMC 2008).
