Shrimp management


The Gulf of Mexico Fishery Management Council manages four species of shrimp. Brown shrimp, white shrimp, and pink shrimp have similar habitat requirements; they begin life in near-shore in estuary environments before moving out to soft bottom and shell habitats in the Gulf of Mexico. Royal red shrimp live in much deeper waters and are known to associate with reef habitats.

The Gulf of Mexico Fishery Management Council manages the shrimp fishery by controlling seasons and area closures. Unlike most of the other species managed by the Council, shrimp, with the exception of royal red shrimp, are not subject to annual catch limits because they are considered to be an annual crop.
